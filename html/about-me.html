About Me

Hello! I’m Brodric.

I am a Computer Engineering graduate from Brigham Young University-Idaho with a deep-rooted passion for hardware engineering. My professional philosophy is built on the belief that the most effective systems are those engineered to be reliable and built to last.

The Hardware-Firmware Bridge

I specialize in the physical layer of engineering. My project experience ranges from analog circuit design and PCB fabrication to implementing low-level firmware for STM32 and ESP32 microcontrollers. I enjoy the full lifecycle of a device starting with schematic capture and board layout, moving into hardware validation, and finishing with the firmware that brings the silicon to life. Whether I am tuning PID control loops or optimizing power consumption, my focus remains on building reliable, performant hardware.

Craftsmanship and Structural Precision

My engineering mindset is shaped as much by the workshop as it is by the lab. I have extensive experience in both fine woodworking and large-scale construction such as heavy-duty framing and cabinet making. I believe there is a direct parallel between the discipline required for structural integrity in a building and the rigor needed for signal integrity in a circuit. Whether I am framing a wall, routing a custom enclosure for a sensor array, or crafting a fine decorative wood project, I prioritize structural precision, clean design, and functional excellence. This hands-on background allows me to not only design the electronics but also build the mechanical frameworks that house them.
